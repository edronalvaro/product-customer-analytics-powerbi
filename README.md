# Product & Customer Analytics Dashboard

## Project Overview

This Power BI project analyzes retail sales performance, product profitability, and customer purchasing behavior using an interactive dashboard.

The goal of this project was to transform raw transactional data into actionable business insights by analyzing revenue trends, customer segments, product performance, and purchasing patterns.

The dashboard provides a high-level executive overview while allowing users to drill into product and customer-level performance.

---

# Business Questions

This analysis focuses on answering:

- How is overall sales performance trending?
- Which products generate the most revenue and profit?
- Which products sell the highest volume but contribute less revenue?
- Which customer segments drive the most value?
- How does customer age and geography impact purchasing behavior?
- Which products may require pricing, inventory, or marketing adjustments?

---

# Dashboard Pages

## 1. Executive Overview

### KPIs

- Total Sales: **$29.35M**
- Total Customers: **18.48K**
- Returning Customers: **15.99K**
- Average Orders Per Customer: **3.27**
- New Customers (Last Period): **12.07K**

---

## Key Insights

### Strong Customer Retention

Returning customers represent the majority of the customer base:

- Returning Customers: **15.99K**
- Total Customers: **18.48K**

This indicates strong customer loyalty and suggests that retention strategies are contributing significantly to revenue generation.

The business should continue investing in:
- Customer loyalty programs
- Personalized marketing campaigns
- Repeat purchase incentives

---

### Customer Segment Revenue Contribution

New customers generate the largest share of revenue:

- New customers contributed approximately **40.18% of total sales ($11.79M)**
- VIP customers contributed approximately **35.94% of total sales ($10.55M)**
- Regular customers contributed approximately **23.88% of total sales ($7.01M)**

Although VIP customers generate significant revenue, new customers currently represent the largest revenue segment. This suggests the business is effective at customer acquisition but should focus on converting new customers into repeat and VIP customers to increase long-term customer value.

Business recommendations:
- Create retention campaigns targeting new customers after their first purchase.
- Develop loyalty programs to increase VIP conversion.
- Analyze purchasing patterns of VIP customers to identify behaviors that drive higher lifetime value.

---

### Geographic Revenue Concentration

The United States represents the largest customer market.

Top customer locations:

1. United States
2. Australia
3. United Kingdom
4. France
5. Germany
6. Canada

The business has strong concentration in English-speaking markets, creating opportunities for localized campaigns in underperforming regions.

# 2. Product Performance Analysis

## Key Insights

### High-Value vs High-Volume Products

The analysis shows that some products generate high revenue while others generate high transaction volume.

Products with high revenue but lower volume indicate premium-priced products with strong profitability potential.

Products with high volume but lower revenue may require:
- Pricing optimization
- Bundle opportunities
- Upselling strategies

---

## Top Products by Revenue

The highest revenue-generating products were primarily Mountain Bike products.

Top performers generated approximately:

- $1.4M+
- $1.3M+
- $1.3M+

These products should receive priority for:
- Inventory availability
- Marketing investment
- Promotional campaigns

---

## Top Products by Quantity Sold

Products with the highest sales volume generated approximately:

- 4K+ units sold

High-volume products represent strong customer demand and should be monitored closely to avoid stock shortages.

---

## Profitability Analysis

Top products by total profit generated approximately:

- $500K-$600K+ profit per product

Revenue leaders were not always the same as profit leaders, highlighting the importance of analyzing margin instead of sales alone.

Business recommendation:

Evaluate products using:
- Revenue
- Quantity sold
- Profit contribution

rather than relying on one metric.

---

## Underperforming Products

Several products generated lower revenue and profit:

- Bottom revenue products generated less than $10K
- Low-performing products contributed limited profitability

Potential actions:

- Review pricing strategy
- Reduce inventory allocation
- Evaluate product demand
- Consider promotional discounts

# 3. Customer Behavior Analysis

## Key Insights

### Customer Segmentation

Customer distribution:

- New Customers: **~80%**
- VIP Customers: **~9%**
- Regular Customers: **~11%**

The majority of customers are new customers, creating a major opportunity for improving retention.

Recommendation:

Convert new customers into repeat customers through:
- Email campaigns
- Loyalty rewards
- Personalized recommendations

---

## Sales by Customer Segment

VIP customers generate the highest sales contribution:

- VIP customers generated approximately **$10.55M**
- Regular customers generated approximately **$7.01M**
- New customers generated approximately **$11.79M**

This shows that both VIP and newly acquired customers are major revenue drivers.

---

## Customer Age Analysis

The highest sales contribution came from customers aged:

1. 40-49
2. 50-59
3. 60+

Older customer groups generate the strongest purchasing power.

Marketing opportunities:

- Target older demographics with premium products
- Develop campaigns focused on quality and long-term value

---

## Average Order Value by Age Group

Highest average order values came from:

- Age group 50-59: ~$1.3K
- Age group 40-49: ~$1.1K

Customers in these age ranges demonstrate stronger purchasing power.

Recommendation:

Focus premium product campaigns toward these demographics.

# Conclusion

This dashboard provides a complete view of retail performance by connecting sales, product, and customer analytics.

The analysis shows that revenue is driven by a combination of high-value products, VIP customers, and mature customer segments.

Key business opportunities include improving customer retention, optimizing product strategy, and focusing marketing efforts on high-value customer groups.
